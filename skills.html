Skills Development

Explore free resources to develop new skills and advance your education

General Learning & Courses

Website Description
Khan Academy Offers free courses in math, science, computer science, history, and more. Great for students K–12 and early college.
Coursera (Free Courses Only) Offers some fully free courses (look for “Audit” or “Free” label). Be cautious—certificates usually cost extra.
edX (Audit Track) Many courses are free if you choose the “Audit” option (no certificate). From institutions like MIT, Harvard, etc.
MIT OpenCourseWare Free course materials from MIT on a huge range of topics including engineering, humanities, and computer science.
OpenLearn (The Open University) A UK-based platform offering 1000+ free courses across all disciplines. No registration required.
Saylor Academy 100% free courses with optional low-cost certificates. Focuses on college-level content.
Academic Earth Free video courses and lectures from top universities in various subjects.
LibreTexts Open-access textbooks and learning resources for science, math, engineering, and more.

Coding & Technology

Website Description
freeCodeCamp Learn coding, web dev, data science, and earn free certifications. Project-based and very popular.
CS50 (Harvard) Harvard’s legendary intro to computer science course available for free.
The Odin Project A free full-stack web development curriculum with no sign-up barriers.
W3Schools Free tutorials for HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, and other web technologies.
MIT OpenCourseWare - CS Deep, university-level CS topics, all free.

Math & Science

Website Description
Paul's Online Math Notes Free math tutorials covering algebra, calculus, and differential equations.
Purplemath Free algebra lessons and problem-solving strategies.
CK-12 Foundation Free digital textbooks and interactive exercises for math and science K–12.
PhET Simulations Free interactive science and math simulations from the University of Colorado Boulder.
NASA STEM Resources Free educational materials from NASA covering space science, technology, and engineering.

Literature, History, Social Sciences

Website Description
Project Gutenberg Over 70,000 free eBooks, including classic literature and historical texts.
Internet Archive Free access to books, lectures, videos, and other educational media.
Library of Congress Resources for teachers, students, and researchers in U.S. history and civics.

Language Learning

Website Description
Duolingo Free gamified language learning in 30+ languages.
BBC Languages (Archived) Free audio, video, and text materials in several languages.
Internet Polyglot Vocabulary training in many languages, entirely free.
